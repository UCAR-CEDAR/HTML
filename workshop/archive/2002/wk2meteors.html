Meteors: Physics, Chemistry and Techniques

Friday 21 June 2002
Silverthorne
01:00-03:00 pm

Convenor: Diego Janches, Arecibo Observatory Qihou Zhou, Arecibo Observatory
  John Mathews, Penn State Univ.  

This workshop focuses on recent advances in the observation of meteors using radar and optical techniques as well as studies the meteoroid interaction with the atmosphere/ionosphere from aeronomic, radio science, and plasma physics viewpoints.

  • Radar and optical observations of meteors
  • Radar scattering mechanisms
  • Electrodymaics of meteor trails
  • Meteoric chemistries
  • Meteoric effects on the ionosphere and the atmosphere (e.g., mass flux)
  • Discussion on the future directions of meteor researches

Those who are interested on presenting a talk, please send a title and a short abstract (less than 100 words) to Diego Janches.
